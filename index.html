Summary

“The measure of intelligence is the ability to change.” — Albert Einstein

Lifelong Agents: Learning, Aligning, and Evolving — AI is at a turning point. Despite rapid progress in reinforcement learning, large language models, and embodied systems, most agents still follow a train once, deploy once paradigm: they excel on benchmarks yet falter when tasks shift, environments drift, or deployments extend over time. This workshop reframes intelligence as a process that learns continuously, aligns reliably with human preferences, and evolves its capabilities responsibly across an agent’s lifespan.

We gather researchers and practitioners from academia and industry to unify fragmented threads—continual learning, agentic RL, post-training for LLMs, alignment, efficiency, and multi-agent collectives—into a shared agenda. Through invited talks, contributed papers, and a panel discussion, we will surface principles, stress-test assumptions, and outline a roadmap for agents that are not just high-performing, but intelligently persistent.

More detail can be found in the original proposal.

Why Lifelong Agents?

We envision agents that accumulate knowledge, refine skills, and expand toolsets over months and years—without catastrophic forgetting or value drift. They remain focused on predefined goals, adapt responsibly to changing constraints, and leverage collective intelligence where appropriate. Progress along this trajectory demands advances in three tightly coupled dimensions:

  • Learning Across Time: continual acquisition and consolidation of knowledge; robust adaptation to non-stationary environments and shifting tasks.
  • Alignment Over Lifespans: preference learning, oversight, and guardrails that maintain value consistency and trustworthiness as agents change.
  • Evolving Capabilities: self-improvement in representations, memory, strategies, and tooling—individually and in multi-agent collectives.

Cross-cutting requirements—efficiency and sustainability—ensure these systems remain practical and responsible at scale.

Call for Papers

Scope & Topics

We invite long (8-page) and short (4-page) paper submissions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Post-training of Agents: continual fine-tuning, instruction alignment, domain-shift adaptation, agentic reinforcement learning.
  • Real-world Agents: multimodal and embodied agents, scientific/enterprise agents, evaluation and benchmarks for real deployments.
  • Preference-Aligned Agents: safety, personalization, value stability over time, efficient and resource-aware agents, self-monitoring/self-awareness.
  • Self-Evolving Agents: memory-augmented agents, autonomous refinement of reasoning and tools, self-improvement loops.
  • Multi-Agent Systems: cooperation, competition, negotiation, coordination, and collective intelligence.
  • Cross-cutting Foundations: efficiency, sustainability, evaluation protocols for lifelong learning and long-horizon alignment.

Submission Instructions

  • Submissions should be anonymized and formatted according to the workshop’s template (PDF). Preprints are welcome.
  • Page limits: 8 pages (long) or 4 pages (short), excluding references and appendices.
  • Review will be double-blind. At least one author of each accepted paper must register and present (oral or poster).
  • We encourage the release of code, data, and evaluation artifacts when feasible.

Detailed policies, templates, and the submission portal will be announced here. More detail can be found in the original proposal.
